The first quarter of 2026 has been the most productive since groundbreaking. Seventy-eight units across Phase One are now fully roofed — a 38% increase from year-end — and foundation work has begun on the first cluster of Phase Two terraces.

This is what monthly progress looks like at Southern Bridge City, written for residents and prospective buyers who want to follow the build without parsing engineering drawings.

## The structural milestone

The Phase One structural envelope is now 89% complete. Reinforced concrete frames are up across all 64 semi-detached residences, and 78 of those have completed roof installations using insulated standing-seam aluminium systems rated for 50-year service.

What this means in practical terms: the buildings are weathertight. From this point forward, internal works can proceed independently of weather. We're already seeing this acceleration in the finishing programme.

## Foundation work in Phase Two

Site clearance and substructure works for the first 24 Phase Two units began in late February. Foundations are reinforced concrete strip on consolidated bedrock, designed to the same specification as Phase One — overengineered relative to what Abuja's geology requires, deliberately.

Phase Two's footprint mirrors Phase One's masterplan, with one notable addition: a central park spine that connects the two phases at the western boundary, designed by landscape architect Tope Ogunbiyi.

## A new partnership with Lafarge

We've finalised an exclusive Phase Two supply agreement with Lafarge Africa for low-carbon concrete. The mix uses 30% supplementary cementitious materials — primarily ground granulated blast furnace slag — which reduces embodied carbon by approximately 25% compared to standard OPC concrete.

The cost difference is roughly 4% over standard mix; we're absorbing it. There are perhaps eight residential developments across Sub-Saharan Africa using this specification at scale. We want Geodata's name on that list.

## What residents can expect through Q2

- **April:** Internal block work and first-fix electrical/plumbing across roofed units
- **May:** External lime render application begins on the first 24 units
- **June:** Show home opens — fully fitted, fully landscaped, available for in-person viewing

The independent quantity surveyors at Adekunle & Partners verify monthly progress against programme. The full April report will be published to all unit holders the first week of May.
